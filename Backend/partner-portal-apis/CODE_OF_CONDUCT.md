# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the Jio Consent Management System(JCMS) project pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on delivering a high-quality, secure, and compliant consent management system for the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 of India.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community and project goals
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being patient with those learning or new to the project
- Sharing knowledge and expertise with others
- Recognizing and crediting the contributions of others
- Promoting privacy-by-design principles and data protection best practices
- Supporting the mission of digital empowerment and citizen data rights

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
- Conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Dismissive or demeaning behavior toward contributors or their contributions
- Sustained disruption of discussions or meetings
- Pattern of inappropriate social contact or unwanted communication
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior
- Attempting to compromise or bypass security measures
- Misusing or exposing sensitive personal data or consent information

## Technical and Professional Standards

In addition to community standards, contributors are expected to maintain high technical and professional standards:

### Code Quality Standards

- Follow established coding standards (Java, ReactJS, Spring Boot conventions)
- Maintain 90% minimum code coverage for all contributions
- Write clear, maintainable, and well-documented code
- Follow the project architecture and design patterns
- Ensure DPDP Act 2023 compliance in all contributions
- Implement security-first approaches in all code changes
- Maintain multi-tenant data isolation and privacy protection

### Development Workflow

- Use clear, descriptive commit messages (Conventional Commits format)
- Follow git flow with feature branches
- Keep pull requests focused and of reasonable size
- Include comprehensive tests and documentation with code changes
- Respond to code review feedback constructively and promptly
- Ensure all commits are signed and verified
- Follow semantic versioning for releases

### Code Review Standards

- Provide constructive, specific, and actionable feedback
- Explain reasoning behind suggestions with focus on security and compliance
- Review code promptly to maintain project velocity
- Focus on code quality, security, performance, maintainability, and DPDP compliance
- Be respectful and professional in all review comments
- Verify that changes maintain backward compatibility where required
- Ensure proper error handling and logging practices

### Security and Compliance

- Never commit secrets, credentials, or sensitive information
- Follow security best practices for data protection and privacy
- Maintain multi-tenant isolation and data privacy at all times
- Report security vulnerabilities through responsible disclosure channels
- Ensure all contributions comply with DPDP Act 2023 requirements
- Implement proper input validation and sanitization
- Use encryption for sensitive data at rest and in transit
- Follow principle of least privilege in access controls

### Documentation Standards

- Maintain up-to-date API documentation
- Include clear installation and configuration instructions
- Document all configuration parameters and environment variables
- Provide examples and use cases for major features
- Keep security documentation current and comprehensive
- Document DPDP compliance features and requirements

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repository and all related repositories
- Issue tracking and project management systems
- Pull requests and code reviews
- Documentation (README, API docs, technical guides)
- All official communication channels (email, forums, chat, discussions)
- Project meetings, conferences, and events
- External representation of the project (conferences, blog posts, social media)
- Government collaboration platforms and official communications

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces, such as using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project team responsible for enforcement at:

**Primary Contact**: Jio.ConsentSupport@ril.com

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All project team members are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

### Process

1. **Report Submission**: Reports can be submitted via email with details of the incident
2. **Acknowledgment**: Reporter will receive acknowledgment within 48 hours
3. **Investigation**: Project maintainers will review the report and gather additional information if needed
4. **Decision**: Decision on appropriate action will be made within 5 business days
5. **Communication**: Reporter and reported party will be informed of the decision
6. **Appeal**: Appeals can be submitted to the Government Oversight Committee within 14 days
7. **Follow-up**: Monitoring to ensure no retaliation occurs

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion and in accordance with government privacy policies. Project maintainers will:

- Keep the reporter's identity confidential
- Not share details publicly without explicit consent
- Only involve maintainers necessary for investigation
- Protect against retaliation
- Follow government protocols for serious violations

## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from project maintainers, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time (typically 7-30 days). This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time (typically 30-90 days). No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community. May include reporting to appropriate government authorities for severe violations.

## Technical Violations

For technical violations (code quality, security issues, etc.) that do not involve interpersonal conduct:

1. **First Occurrence**: Feedback and guidance provided in code review with educational resources
2. **Repeated Issues**: Discussion with maintainers about expectations and additional training
3. **Persistent Problems**: Temporary restriction on contribution privileges (30-60 days)
4. **Severe Cases**: Permanent restriction if quality/security standards cannot be met
5. **Security Violations**: Immediate escalation to security team and possible legal review

## Special Considerations for Government Context

### Legal Compliance
- All activities must comply with applicable Indian laws and regulations
- Contributors may be subject to background verification for sensitive contributions
- Violations affecting national security or data protection may be escalated to appropriate authorities

### International Contributors
- International contributors are welcome but must comply with Indian data protection laws
- Export control laws may apply to certain technical contributions
- All contributions must align with Indian digital sovereignty principles
